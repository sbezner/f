How to get influencers

A practical guide for pre-launch outreach — no product on shelves yet, no ad budget required.

Start with micro-influencers

Target creators with 10k–100k followers, not mega-influencers. Micro-influencers have higher engagement, post more authentically, and will actually respond to a cold outreach. A creator with 2M followers won't touch a product that doesn't exist yet.

Who to target specifically

  • Dermatologist and esthetician creators on TikTok — they explain ingredients to large audiences and their endorsement carries clinical weight
  • Fitness and wellness women who already talk about amino acids, pre-workout, or supplements — they understand the mechanism without being taught
  • Clean beauty reviewers on Instagram and Substack who have given up on plumpers due to sensitivity
Best platforms

TikTok for discovery, Instagram Reels for conversion, Substack for long-form credibility

Follower sweet spot

10k–100k. High trust, lower cost, more likely to post organically about something genuinely interesting

How to find them

  • Search TikTok and Instagram for #lipplumper, #cleanbeauty, #dermatologist — look at who's posting, not who's being tagged
  • Creator.co and AspireIQ — free to browse, filter by category and follower count
  • TikTok Creator Marketplace — free account, search by niche and audience
  • Manual DM outreach on Instagram — still works and feels more personal at this stage

The ask at this stage

Don't pay anyone yet. You're pre-launch — that's actually a selling point. Send the site link, explain the mechanism in one sentence, and ask if they'd want to be among the first to try it when samples are ready.

The nitric oxide story is genuinely interesting to derm creators. Lead with the science angle, not the product. "We use the same amino acids athletes take for blood flow, but applied directly to the lip" is a hook that writes itself.

Cold DM template

Copy and send

"Hey [name] — I've been following your content and love how you break down ingredients for your audience.

I'm building a lip plumper that works completely differently from anything on the market — it uses the nitric oxide pathway (same one athletes use for blood flow) instead of capsicum or menthol, so there's zero burn. Mechanism is real, and I think your audience would find the science genuinely interesting.

We're pre-launch and I'm looking for a small group of founding voices to be first to try it. No ask, no script — just want people who'll give me an honest reaction. Would you be open to it?"

The test that matters most

When you have samples, send 100 unbranded units to micro-influencers — no payment, no NDA, no requested post. Just the product and a brief explanation of how it works.

If less than 8% post about it organically, the product isn't talkable enough. That number tells you more than any paid post or focus group. Fix the product or the story before spending on marketing.

What to track

  • Unsolicited post rate on the seed send (target: ≥8%)
  • Comment sentiment — are people asking where to buy?
  • Saves and shares, not just likes — saves signal intent to purchase
  • Any creator who posts unpaid is a candidate for a paid founding partnership later
